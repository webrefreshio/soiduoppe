## The Language of Warning Lights

Modern vehicles communicate through a series of dashboard warning lights. Understanding these signals helps you respond appropriately and prevent costly damage.

## Critical Warning Lights (Stop Immediately)

### Brake System Warning

Indicates low brake fluid, worn pads, or hydraulic issues. Stop safely and have the system inspected immediately.

### Engine Temperature Warning

Overheating can cause severe engine damage. Pull over safely, turn off the engine, and call for assistance.

### Oil Pressure Warning

Low oil pressure can destroy your engine in minutes. Stop immediately and check oil levels.

## Important Warning Lights (Service Soon)

### Check Engine Light

Can indicate many issues from minor to serious. Requires diagnostic scan to determine the cause.

### Tire Pressure Monitoring

Low tire pressure affects safety and fuel efficiency. Check and inflate tires to proper pressure.

### Battery/Charging System

Indicates alternator or battery issues. Have the charging system tested.

## Maintenance Reminder Lights

### Oil Change Indicator

Time for routine oil and filter change. Follow your manufacturer's recommended intervals.

### Service Required

General maintenance reminder. Check your owner's manual for specific service requirements.

### Brake Pad Wear

Time to inspect brake pads and possibly replace them.
